1. Introduction

Audio Recordings: Single-file compilation of Lanza’s videos, in order of upload dates.

CulturalPhilistine was a YouTube channel that was owned and operated by Adam Lanza. On 2012 December 14, Lanza murdered his mother and 26 people at Sandy Hook Elementary School before committing suicide.

NOTE: A few weeks after I created this page, I discovered that Peter F. Langman had created an improved transcript of Lanza’s videos, complete with screenshots, video descriptions, view counts, like and dislike totals, footnotes, etc. I would say that it’s mostly better than the version that I created on this page. However this version of the transcripts is in HTML format, has an arguably better font and margins, a hyperlinked TOC, and some improvements/differences in the transcription quality that currently aren’t in Langman’s December 2024 version. An ultimate copy of the transcripts would probably combine the best aspects of both transcripts together. It’d also be helpful if the transcripts would indicate every pause that occurs within the recordings.

This page presents an improved version of the Cultural Philistine video transcripts shown here. Unfortunately, the original transcripts at that URL have some typos, spacing problems, and no TOC. Hopefully, people will find this copy of the transcripts to be an improvement. According to the video descriptions, Lanza released all of his YouTube videos under the CC BY 3.0 license.

All of these videos are listed in the order that they were created by Adam Lanza (with the exception of how all three strange dream videos are placed consecutively, but this does not affect comprehension). Some archive collections list and automatically play the videos in alphabetical order or some other non-chronological order. However, I recommend watching the videos in chronological order, since he sometimes refers to things from his previous videos in his later videos.

For the eight videos where Lanza narrates and talks about his pedophilia essay, I recommend reading the revised version of Adam Lanza’s pedophilia essay, as hosted on this site. This revision was intended to finish the original essay, and it’s easier to read, follow, and comprehend, compared to listening and reading these text transcripts or the original (unfinished) essay itself.

2. “My Antinatalism”

(09/07/2011)

Adam Lanza: “I didn’t understand this when I was younger, but I’ve always had an immense hatred for culture. I consider culture to be delusional values which humans mindlessly coerce onto each other, spreading it no differently than any other disease. I previously sought to eliminate my cultural values to the greatest extent that I could. Through this I expected to gradually discover values of an inner-self which could be reconciled with this society, so that I could engage in activities and pursue goals which would lead to happiness.”

“Eventually I got to a point where I had sufficiently freed myself from what I called ‘cultural values’ when I analyzed all of the things which brought me happiness and all the goals which I wanted to pursue, I realized that absolutely everything about those things that appealed to me was entirely a consequence of my cultural infection. Formerly, I had rejected some aspects of culture while accepting other ones, and merely not calling them cultural as if those values were somehow transcendent and fine. It was at that point that I realized that there is no such thing as an inner self. Any sense of self is a delusional cultural construct. I realized that cultural infections were the sole source of any possible value beyond base values. For a while, I believed that happiness could be attained if culture could theoretically be eradicated and anarcho[sic]-anarcho primitivism were to take hold. Replace all instances of technology with culture in the analytic sections of Industrial Society and Its Future (Ted Kaczynski, 1995) and you basically have my mentality at the time regarding the pernicious effects of culture.”

“The problem was that I’d not been addressing what happiness is. Happiness is merely the fulfillment of value, I recognized that if cultural values were eliminated, the happiness which results from their fulfillment would not be needed because happiness becomes an unnecessary and incoherent concept when it is removed from its’ concep[sic]-context. A common theme in my quasi-Anarcho Primitivist thought at the time is that non-base values exist only as a consequence of cultural infections, and impede on the happiness which results from the fulfillment of feral values. As if [?] my feral self is - in short metaphysically the real me, whose soul had been devoured by the culturally constructed imposter of the self. But my feral self was also an imposter. Just as I realized that I could eliminate non-base values and have no need for the happiness which resulted from their fulfillment, I could eliminate base-values and have no need for the happiness which resulted from their fulfillment.”

“It was not only the disease of culture that had been plaguing me all- all along, it was the disease of life itself. I was not and I am not in some existential crisis, I’ve never had the slightest problem with the obvious nonexistence of free will, objective purposes, and all of that. I have always been entirely psychologically capable of accepting my own subjective values and goals, even though I know they are consummately inconsequential, and it doesn’t bother me at all.”

“The problem is not that I seek meaning and cannot find it. The problem is that I do feel immense meaning, and so does everyone else who is alive. Meaning is an abstract interpretation of value which exists only because of life. Just as I sought to eradicate the delusional values which culture infected me with, the final solution is the termination of my life, to rid myself of all value. The solution cannot be to embrace some aspect of life, as if the erosion of delusions is the cause of this. Life is what urgently caused me to have value, and changing my life will never do anything but create different delusions than the ones I already have. Unfortunately as of right now I lack the discipline to pursue my death and to rid myself of the values which delude me, even though I recognize [that] the solution to life is death. But I do commend others who do commit suicide. They have freed themselves from culture, life and all value. They have freed themselves from themselves.”
