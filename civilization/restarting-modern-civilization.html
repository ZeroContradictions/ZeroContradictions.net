1. Modern Civilization Has Been Going Downhill Ever Since The 2000s Started

For most of the entire world, I’d say that the 80s and 90s were the best time period ever. The Soviet Union was falling apart / had fallen, most of the world’s economies had ascending economic growth, there was great culture being produced, it still seemed as if there was lots of technological progress to be made, the world didn’t have the dysgenics and overpopulation problems that it does now, etc.

Everything got so bad after the 1990s: The War on Terror, the Great Recession, the rise of China as a superpower, increasing overpopulation and dysgenics, mass cultural insanity, Trump vs Clinton, unprecedented political partisanship, we’ve almost reached peak technology (and thus there will be hardly anything new left to discover or invent), COVID-19 lockdowns, George Floyd Riots, Russia invades Ukraine, global recession, etc.

By comparison, the future seemed so bright in the 90s. The USSR just fell, the economy was in great shape, there were still many advancements in computing and technology to be made, there was no LGBT / BLM crap, and the West had a more sane, conservative culture. Everything went to shit after that.

3. What It Would Be Like To Restart Modern Human Civilization If It Falls

Instead of a sudden collapse, humanity will most likely experience a long decline, with some shocks that take us down to lower levels of complex civilization quickly, but not all the way down. It will probably be like a series of steps, and it will differ by region.

There are a lot of unknowns. We cannot predict the future in the sense of predicting a sequence of events, but we do know that the current system is unstable, and so we can predict that it will collapse, unless it is reformed in certain ways.

The end of civilization near the end of the 21st century will feature worldwide overpopulation by a degenerated genome, many cities and societies destroyed by wars caused by said disastrous overpopulation, global water shortages, global sand shortages (since a particular type of sand is necessary to build semiconductors and is in ever-decreasing supply), untold amounts of urban decay, and so on.

Annual heat waves will become more widespread, feature record breaking temperatures year after year, will render many regions as uninhabitable, and start to become a major cause for population reduction, unless the world can somehow alter the atmosphere’s composition to block out more sunlight and bring the Earth back to previous temperatures.

Some Other Differences If We Must Make A Second Attempt To Start Modern Civilization:

  • White people would be very rare across the world since the Great Replacement would deplete the Earth’s European population.
  • Fossil fuels would be all gone (especially all the ones that were the easiest to access)
  • Humans would have to live in a world of considerably less diversity in flora and fauna, due to the Sixth Recorded Mass Extinction Event.
  • Humans have already undergone the encephalization process historically, so even if all the biggest game animals are all gone, this wouldn’t be a problem as long as a stable food source that provides all the necessary nutrients is available to some degree.
  • The Earth would be considerably hotter than anything that modern humans have endured during Earth’s history due to climate change, which would negatively impact human survival.
  • Humans may be more likely to act differently, if somehow knowledge of the past before the Great Collapse (the reverse Industrial Revolution) occurred. And this knowledge could enable the humans that retain that historical knowledge to act in ways that might avoid some of history’s greatest mistakes.
  • The Great Exchange between the Old World and the New World has already happened, so old world and new world flora, fauna, and crops would still be present on all the continents as long as the people living in their respective places continue to cultivate them.
  • Although many viral and bacterial diseases like polio, tuberculosis, measles, small pox, and more have been either eliminated or greatly reduced due to modern vaccination, those diseases and maybe some others might come back. If it is the case that diseases continue to exist worldwide to some extent or another and that humanity is unable to sufficiently evolve to it without vacciation, then human populations worldwide will be limited by disease instead of war, which would be somewhat more preferable since war entails violence, destruction of property, increased rape, and other consequences that are more unfavorable than the ones entailed by sufficiently widespread disease.
  • There may be more human genetic diversity in every region around the world due to massive race mixing that occurred during the 21st century. This could have some positive benefits, but it would probably be a slight barrier to restarting modern civilization since it would cause populations all around the world to be less adapted to their pre-industrial environments.
  • More violent genes would be selected for during and after the Great Collapse since those genes would be more adaptive in an environment where the tremendous cooperation that once held Civilization together has completely ceased on the scale that it once did. This would make it more difficult to restart Civilization.
  • All the nuclear power plants would melt down since they could no longer be maintained, if any were still kept running during the Great Collapse.
