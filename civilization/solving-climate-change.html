1. The Most Ideal Way For Humanity To Solve Climate Change

The most ideal solution to climate change would be to have a global government that regulates how much the atmosphere can be altered and in what way(s) since various countries would very likely have differing opinions on how the atmosphere should change since it would affect ocean depth levels (and thus affect coastal lands), would affect climates and crop types/growths around the world, and much more.

2. The Most Likely Way How Humanity Will Solve Climate Change

Since we don’t have a global government for effectively regulating and enforcing pollution and CO2 emission policies, the best solution to global warming (in the absence of a global government) would probably be to alter the Earth’s atmosphere to block out more heat / sunlight, which would lower the global temperature to counter the rising temperatures of recent decades. Without a global government, any attempt to do things like climate pledges will succumb to a global Tragedy of the Commons. That’s why the best solution we have now will be to block slightly more sunlight out of the atmosphere, since it could reduce global warming without facing game-theoretical obstacles to its implementation.

Doing what we can to prevent climate change is a no-brainer. The Earth has been facing record-breaking temperatures for the past two centuries thanks to the changes in the atmosphere caused by the Industrial Revolution. And since humans evolved during the Ice Age when the planet was much cooler, it makes sense that we would want to keep the Earth at a cooler temperature than the temperatures that we are predicting decades from now.

We will have to be careful about this since technology in general is unpredictable and this technology has never been tried before, but if we don’t do this, the Earth’s climate and environment is going to continue to negatively change to the detriment of us humans and all other living species. Fortunately though, we have observed the effects of how sending tons of volcanic ash into the Earth’s atmosphere can cause global temperatures to drop by blocking out sunlight, so we at least have some idea of how to go about this and some of the possible outcomes.

Although this proposal would end or even reverse global warming, it might not completely prevent climate and environmental change. One example is that since the quantity of carbon-dioxide in the atmosphere and oceans would continue to increase, coral reefs would still be harmed by the increased acidity of the ocean water, although they would still benefit from global temperatures no longer increasing or even decreasing. That being said, a carbon tax would still be reasonable, even if the atmosphere was altered to block out more sunlight. Developed countries that have already emitted a lot of carbon dioxide should be taxed more since they’ve already made their share of pollution.

4. The Future Of Energy Will Probably Be Nuclear

Nuclear energy is a clean source of abundant, reliable energy that produces no carbon emissions.

However, a potential problem with increasing the production of nuclear energy around the world is that it could be a gateway for many countries to start acquiring nuclear weapons, if they haven’t already done so.
